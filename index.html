States with Abortion Bans Face Growing Doctor Deficit and Maternal Health Risks

Quinn Waller
Dec. 22, 2024

States with abortion restrictions generally see fewer OB-GYNs per capita, raising concerns about women’s ability to access healthcare.

In light of the recent Supreme Court decision in Dobbs v. Jackson, which overturned the constitutional right of access to abortion, states with abortion restrictions have seen a decrease in the number of residency applications, according to a study by the American Association of Medical Colleges.

This decrease of doctors in states with abortion restrictions “may negatively affect access to care in those states,” the study found.

Data from the AAMC and the U.S. Census showed that states with abortion bans or restrictions generally had fewer OB-GYNs per woman over the age of 15 than states without restrictions, with the exception of Louisiana.

Some doctors are concerned about the impact this scarcity has on women’s healthcare generally.

“I worry about the worsening shortages and health disparities, and that this will deepen the maternal mortality crisis we are facing in this country,” said maternal-fetal medicine specialist Kylie Cooper, in an interview with the AAMC.
